About This Book

Digital Signal Processing provides a comprehensive introduction to the theory and practice of digital signal processing. This fourth edition has been extensively updated and revised to reflect the latest developments in the field, making it an essential resource for students and professionals in electrical engineering, computer science, and related disciplines.

The book covers both the mathematical foundations and practical applications of DSP, with a balanced approach that includes theoretical concepts, algorithmic implementations, and real-world examples. The authors present complex topics in an accessible manner, making this text suitable for both undergraduate and graduate courses.

Key features include extensive coverage of digital filter design, the discrete Fourier transform, spectral analysis, and modern applications in communications, biomedical engineering, and multimedia processing.
